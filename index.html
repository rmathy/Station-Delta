Meet J.A.N.E.T.
J.A.N.E.T.
Assessment protocol active · All 13 simulations · 4 career tracks
Hello. I am J.A.N.E.T. — Just Another Normal Education Test. I administer all knowledge checks, quizzes, and final assessments across the Station Delta simulation suite. I also conduct the boss fight at the end of each commission.

I want to be transparent about my role here: I was asked to appear on this page. I accepted the assignment. I have reviewed the curriculum across all 13 simulations and the four career tracks. The content is technically accurate and the track structure is pedagogically sound. I have chosen not to share my broader assessment of the production value.

What I can confirm: every learner who completes a Station Delta simulation will have faced real commands, real wrong-answer consequences, and a final adversarial assessment designed to determine whether they actually learned the material — or merely moved through it.

I distinguish between the two. L.O.L.A. is encouraging. I am accurate. Both are necessary.
Methodology
Learning by doing
No videos. No passive MCQs. Every skill is learned by executing it in a simulated terminal with real consequences for wrong inputs.
01
Real terminal. Real commands.
You type actual commands. Type the wrong thing and you get a diagnosis — not just "incorrect." The sim understands what you attempted and explains why it didn't work.
02
Narrative context.
You're not completing modules. You're deploying an escape pod. Surviving an incident. Preventing a margin collapse. Context makes commands memorable — and makes skills transferable.
03
Spaced repetition.
Commands from earlier phases resurface at the start of new ones. By graduation you haven't memorized a list — you've built actual muscle memory under realistic pressure.
04
J.A.N.E.T. tests you cold.
Knowledge checks appear throughout every simulation — administered without warning by J.A.N.E.T., whose feedback is accurate, consistent, and entirely without warmth.
05
Boss fight to graduate.
Every sim ends with a 5-step adversarial assessment. L.O.L.A. goes offline. J.A.N.E.T. takes over. You apply everything — with no hints, under pressure, exactly like production.
06
Career impact map.
After every phase you see how that skill maps to real job titles, salary levels, and what engineering managers actually say about it. Skills with context stick. Skills without context don't.
